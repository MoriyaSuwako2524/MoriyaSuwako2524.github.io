2. Non-inertial frames

(a) Consider a system with Lagrangian L[qα,q˙α] which satisfies the Euler–Lagrange equations

(3)ddt∂L∂q˙α−∂L∂qα=0.

Now assume q can be expressed in terms of a different set of coordinates sα via a time-dependent coordinate transformation
qα=qα(s1,…,sm,t) , where α=1,…,m .

(4)ddt∂L∂s˙α−∂L∂sα=0.

(This should be close to the story for time-independent coordinate changes.)

(b) A bead with mass m is constrained to move on a massless hoop of radius a with a diameter fixed to lie along the z -axis.
The hoop is rotating about the z axis with constant angular speed ω .

i. Lagrangian and equations of motion. Obtain the Lagrangian and the Euler–Lagrange equations of motion assuming that the only external force arises from a constant gravitational force −mgz^ .

ii. Critical angular speed. Show that if ω>ω0 for some particular critical frequency ω0 , there exists a solution in which the particle remains stationary on the hoop at a point other than the bottom (or top), but for ω<ω0 , the only stationary points are the bottom and top. Find this value of ω0 .

Important hints

  • It is worth working in spherical coordinates, with the origin fixed at the center of the hoop, θ the angle from the z axis, and ϕ the azimuthal angle with respect to the x axis. Start with a particle in 3 dimensions (with the gravitational force) in these coordinates, and then impose the constraints.
  • Some of the constraints will be time dependent. Nonetheless, you can still take the resulting time-dependent solution and plug it back into the Lagrangian. The usual Euler–Lagrange equations still follow from stationarity of the action even if the Lagrangian has explicit time dependence.
