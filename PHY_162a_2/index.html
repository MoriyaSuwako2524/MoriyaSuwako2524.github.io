(b) A bead with mass $m$ is constrained to move on a massless hoop of radius $a$ with a diameter fixed to lie along the $z$-axis.
The hoop is rotating about the $z$ axis with constant angular speed $\omega$.

i. Lagrangian and equations of motion. Obtain the Lagrangian and the Euler–Lagrange equations of motion assuming that the only external force arises from a constant gravitational force $-mg\hat z$.

ii. Critical angular speed. Show that if $\omega>\omega_0$ for some particular critical frequency $\omega_0$, there exists a solution in which the particle remains stationary on the hoop at a point other than the bottom (or top), but for $\omega<\omega_0$, the only stationary points are the bottom and top. Find this value of $\omega_0$.

Important hints

  • It is worth working in spherical coordinates, with the origin fixed at the center of the hoop, $\theta$ the angle from the $z$ axis, and $\phi$ the azimuthal angle with respect to the $x$ axis. Start with a particle in 3 dimensions (with the gravitational force) in these coordinates, and then impose the constraints.
  • Some of the constraints will be time dependent. Nonetheless, you can still take the resulting time-dependent solution and plug it back into the Lagrangian. The usual Euler–Lagrange equations still follow from stationarity of the action even if the Lagrangian has explicit time dependence.
